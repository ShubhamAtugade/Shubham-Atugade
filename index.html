Crime Rate analysis and prediction model using Machine learning

This paper investigates the use of machine learning to analyze and predict crime rates in Indian metropolitan cities, achieving a notable accuracy of 93.20% with the Random Forest algorithm. By leveraging extensive datasets from the National Crime Rate Bureau (NCRB), the study emphasizes the importance of meticulous data preprocessing, including cleaning and feature engineering, to ensure the reliability of the predictive model. The findings highlight how machine learning can uncover complex patterns in crime data that traditional methods may overlook, providing valuable insights for law enforcement and urban planning.

The research also discusses the implications of these findings for proactive crime prevention strategies and resource allocation. Future research directions include exploring advanced deep learning techniques, integrating diverse data sources such as social media and economic indicators, and developing real-time prediction systems. Additionally, the paper underscores the need to address ethical considerations surrounding predictive technologies to ensure responsible usage. Overall, this study demonstrates the transformative potential of machine learning in enhancing public safety initiatives and adapting to the evolving dynamics of urban crime.
